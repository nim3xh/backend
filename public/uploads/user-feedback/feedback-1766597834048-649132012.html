FAQs

Answers to real trader concerns

These are written for the questions traders actually ask: compatibility, rules risk, expectations, and how to use each tool without confusion.

Dr. Markets

Is Dr. Markets an indicator or a planning tool?

Dr. Markets is a planning and context tool. It is designed to help you frame the session (structure, volatility posture, zones) so your decisions are deliberate instead of reactive.

Does it tell me exactly when to buy and sell?

No. It is designed to improve decision quality and reduce noise. It supports trading judgment rather than replacing it with constant alerts.

Does it work for ES, NQ, and GC?

Yes. The workflow is designed around U.S. futures use cases, with ES, NQ, and GC as primary examples.

How do I use it day-to-day?

Use it as a short pre-session read, reference it during the session, and archive the output for post-session review.

TradeRx

Is TradeRx compatible with NinjaTrader?

TradeRx is designed around NinjaTrader execution workflows. You configure it to match your preferred session constraints and risk posture.

Will this violate prop firm rules?

Prop firms differ. The intent is rules-aware behavior and consistency. You should configure TradeRx conservatively to align with your specific firm’s constraints and permitted trading methods.

Does TradeRx guarantee passing evaluations?

No. Prop Trader Pro is a system for consistent behavior and review. Results vary based on settings, markets, and trader discipline.

What should I show as “proof” on this page?

Show execution logs and session behavior artifacts. Redact account numbers. Focus on routine, consistency, and review clarity rather than implying guaranteed outcomes.

JournalX

Is JournalX only for automation users?

No. Manual traders and system traders both benefit. The journaling structure is about capturing context, decisions, mistakes, and review outcomes.

Do I need to journal every trade?

No. JournalX is designed for sustainability. Even a weekly review with consistent tags and notes can produce meaningful improvements.

What is the main benefit?

Faster learning cycles. It shortens the gap between making a mistake and fixing it by making patterns obvious and reviewable.

How do I connect JournalX to the rest of the workflow?

Use Dr. Markets for context, TradeRx for clean execution artifacts, then JournalX for review and pattern discovery.

How to read a futures session like a professional

This is an on-page mini version. Replace with your real article later.

A professional session read is less about prediction and more about posture. Before the first trade, you want a clear answer to three questions: What kind of day is this? Where do decisions matter? Where do trades become low-quality noise?

For ES, NQ, and GC, the “middle of nowhere” is where discipline dies. Your goal is to define decision points—zones where the market either accepts price or rejects it—and only take risk when the market is actually making a decision.

Use structure zones to prevent early-session overconfidence. If the market is choppy and mean-reverting, you trade smaller or trade less. If the market is trending cleanly, you stop fighting it and focus on higher-quality entries.

Volatility regimes and why traders feel out of sync

Replace with your real article later.

When traders feel “out of sync,” the market has usually changed regime. A strategy that works in smooth conditions fails in noisy conditions, and the trader interprets that failure as personal incompetence.

Your job is to recognize the regime early, then adapt risk and expectations. In higher volatility, reduce size, widen expectations, and become more selective. In lower volatility, avoid forcing trades that need movement that is not there.

Structural zones: where your best trades actually live

Replace with your real article later.

Most losing prop behavior happens between meaningful prices. Structural zones are areas where the market has a reason to decide: prior highs and lows, failed breaks, acceptance zones, and areas of repeated rejection.

When you anchor your trading to zones, you naturally trade less. You stop “hunting” and start waiting for a decision. That shift alone eliminates many low-quality entries.
